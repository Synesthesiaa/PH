Batanes is a group of islands located in the northernmost part of the Philippines. It is composed of ten islands, with three inhabited islands namely Batan, Sabtang, and Itbayat. Batanes is known for its scenic landscapes, unique Ivatan culture, and well-preserved traditional stone houses.

The islands of Batanes are characterized by rolling hills, rugged coastline, and crystal-clear waters. Tourists can enjoy activities such as hiking, biking, beach hopping, and cultural immersion. Some of the popular tourist spots in Batanes include the Basco Lighthouse, Marlboro Country, Valugan Boulder Beach, and Chawa View Deck.

Aside from its natural beauty, Batanes is also famous for its rich cultural heritage. The Ivatan people, who are the indigenous inhabitants of the islands, have a distinct culture and way of life. They have their own language, cuisine, and unique customs that are different from other ethnic groups in the Philippines. Some of the notable cultural landmarks in Batanes include the Ivatan houses, traditional stone churches, and the traditional Ivatan headgear known as vakul.

Batanes is considered as one of the most scenic destinations in the Philippines, and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1993 for its cultural landscape and traditions.

CULTURE OF BATANES

TRADITION- BATANES

Locals still wear traditional articles of clothing like the vakul, a headpiece made of fibers from the voyavoy palm, which is used to protect the wearer from sun and rain. The stone houses of Batanes are built to withstand typhoons, with walls and roofs one meter thick, and small and narrow windows.

BELIEFS- BATANES

Most Ivatans are Catholics, like the rest of the country, although some have not converted and practice ancestral worship to their anitos. However, there are growing Protestant denominations especially in Basco, the capital town of Batanes.

ARTS(Basco Lighthouse)- BATANES

The Basco Lighthouse is a famous landmark in Batanes, with a unique design that reflects the Ivatan culture. It is a popular spot for tourists and photographers.

ARTS(Ivatan houses)- BATANES

The Ivatan houses are unique structures made of stone walls and thatched roofs. They are built to withstand typhoons and earthquakes and are considered a significant cultural heritage of Batanes.

ARTS(Vakul)- BATANES

Vakul - a traditional headgear made of abaca fibers that is worn by the Ivatan people, especially during rainy weather. It is woven into intricate designs and serves as a protection from the rain and sun.
