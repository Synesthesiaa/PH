Bohol is an island province located in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. It is the tenth largest island in the country and is known for its unique geological features, such as the Chocolate Hills, which are a series of cone-shaped hills that turn brown during the dry season, giving them the appearance of chocolate mounds.

Bohol is also home to a number of historic sites and cultural landmarks, including the 16th-century Baclayon Church, which is considered one of the oldest stone churches in the country, and the Blood Compact Shrine, which commemorates the first treaty of friendship between a Filipino chieftain and a Spanish explorer.

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Bohol is also popular for its beaches and water activities, such as snorkeling and diving, due to its clear waters and diverse marine life.

CULTURE OF BOHOL

TRADITION - BOHOL

Boholanos are very family-oriented. They value solidarity and togetherness, like going to church together during Sundays. They open their doors to guests, give their warmest welcome gestures, prepare their finest chinawares never used on ordinary days, and serve for-guest-only food.

BELIEFS - BOHOL

Catholicism was introduced to Bohol by Spanish colonizers in the 16th century, and it has since become a significant part of the local culture. Many Boholanos practice Catholicism and celebrate religious festivals such as the Holy Week and Christmas.

ARTS(Tarsier sculptures) - BOHOL

The tarsier is a small primate that is native to Bohol. Local artisans often create sculptures of this animal using materials such as wood and clay.

ARTS(Loom weaving) - BOHOL

A traditional craft that has been passed down through generations of Boholano weavers. It involves using a manual loom to create intricate patterns and designs on fabric

ARTS(Buri palm weaving) - BOHOL

The buri palm is a type of plant that grows abundantly in Bohol. Local artisans use its leaves to create a variety of products, such as hats, bags, and baskets.
