Have a wonderful time on your journey and discovering more different cultures.

Known as the “Surfing Capital of the Philippines”, Siargao is mainly responsible for introducing surfing to the country. Apart from surfing, Siargao is also open to other activities such as cave explorations and rock climbing.

Siargao Island is greatly influenced by the winds and currents coming uninterrupted from the Pacific Ocean, intensified by the Mindanao current running westward through the Siargao Strait. The tide of Siargao is diurnal, with tidal curves typically present, especially on the east coast of the island.

CULTURE OF SIARGAO

TRADITION- SIARGAO

Surfing has become a popular activity in Siargao in recent years, but it has always been a part of the island's culture. The locals have been surfing for generations, and they have their own unique style and techniques.

BELIEFS
- SIARGAO

The locals are very welcoming and friendly, happily sharing their culture and traditions. They usually speak the native dialect, Surigaonon, but they understand both English and Filipino very well.

ARTS(Mat Weaving)
- SIARGAO

Mat weaving is a traditional handicraft in Siargao, and the mats are used for sleeping, seating, and covering floors.

ARTS(Kudlit Tattooing)
- SIARGAO

Kudlit is a traditional form of tattooing that originated in the Philippines. It involves using a bamboo needle to create small dots and lines on the skin, which form a pattern or design. In Siargao, kudlit tattooing is still practiced by some of the older members of the community.

ARTS(Bolo Making)
- SIARGAO

Bolos are traditional Filipino knives, and the locals of Siargao are skilled in making them using local materials like bamboo and steel.
